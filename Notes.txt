Notes on EDA: 

1. we have huge imbalance in the dataset for the target_var (Class imbalance: 644 Yes vs 3,596 No → roughly 85% / 15% split)
    - This means we will need to account for this imbalance during the evaluation phase (don't report accuracy, confusion matrix)

2. glucose has the biggest imputation challenge ->  9.2% missing, and likely not missing at random (MNAR) 
since diabetic/high-risk patients may have more measurements taken.

3. bp_meds (blood pressure medications) — very skewed (124 Yes vs 4063 No) 
4. prevalent_stroke — very rare (25 Yes) — may have limited predictive power.
5. diabetes — rare (109 Yes) — potential overlap with glucose. (MNAR)
6. cigs_per_day - may need zero-inflation treatment (heavily right-skewed (many zeros))
7. to handle outliers, try to use cross-check suspicious values against other variables (e.g. is glucose 394 paired with diabetes = Yes?)
